Kaju Katli

Kaju katri (literally "cashew slice"), also known as kaju barfi, is an Indian dessert similar to a barfi. Kaju means cashew; Barfi is often, but not always, made by thickening milk with sugar and other ingredients (such as dry fruits and mild spices). Kesar kaju katli is a kaju katli recipe that includes saffron. The dish is prepared with cashew nuts soaked in water for a considerable period of time (usually overnight), which are then ground to a paste. Sugar solution is boiled down until a single thread forms when two fingers are dipped into it and pulled apart, after which it is added to the ground cashews kaju. Ghee, saffron (kesar), and dried fruits may also be added. The paste is then spread and flattened in a shallow, flat-bottomed dish and cut into bite-sized diamond-shaped pieces. The pieces are usually decorated with edible silver foil. The finished sweet is usually white or yellow in color depending on the ingredients used for the paste and the proportions of each used. Katli is traditionally eaten during Diwali.

instructions

step 1

Firstly, in a mixi take 2 cup cashew and blend to a fine powder. make sure to pulse and blend else, the cashew will release oil and turns into a paste.

step 2

sieve the cashew powder making sure there are no lumps. keep aside

step 3

in a large kadai take 1 cup sugar and Â½ cup water.

step 4

stir well and dissolve sugar. boil for 5 minutes or until 1 string consistency is attained.

step 5

add in powdered cashew and mix well.

step 6

stir continuously until the mixture is well combined and smooth paste is formed.

step 7

now add 1 tsp ghee and Â¼ tsp cardamom powder. mix well.

step 8

continue to cook until the mixture turns smooth paste and starts to separate pan slightly. do not overcook, as the burfi will turn hard.

step 9

transfer the mixture on to the butter paper. make sure to grease the butter paper with ghee.

step 10

now fold using a spatula until the mixture thickens forming a dough.

step 11

once the dough is formed, knead slightly, to form a soft dough.

step 12

place the cashew dough between butter paper and roll using a rolling pin.

step 13

roll slightly thick making sure it's uniform.

step 14

now grease with ghee and apply silver leaf or silver vark. applying vark is optional.

step 15

now cut into diamond shape or shape of your choice

step 16

finally, enjoy kaju katli for a month when stored in an airtight container.
